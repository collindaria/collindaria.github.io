Meet the Bride and Groom

Two paths crossed when Daria visited the University of Utah in February, 2014. Collin and Daria spent all of their spare time together over Daria's recruitment weekend and stayed in contact when Daria returned to Duke. Within weeks, they had decided to take a trip to Tanzania together to climb Mt Kilimanjaro, and by May, 2014, Collin had helped Daria move across the country to begin her life in Utah. Collin and Daria successfully summited Mt Kilimanjaro, the world's tallest freestanding mountain, in August, 2014. In December 2014, Collin proposed marriage with an engagement ring featuring a tanzanite center stone that was mined from the foothills of Mt Kilimanjaro. Collin and Daria currently work in the same laboratory in the University of Utah Bioengineering department under Dr. Alan Dorval, where they collaborate on neural engineering research.

Her




Daria was born in Moscow, Russia, where she lived until she moved to northern Virginia at four years of age. Daria grew up in the suburbs of Washington D.C. and attended Duke University for her undergrad, where she grew to love scientific research while studying deep brain stimulation for the treatment of Parkinson's Disease with Dr. Warren Grill. The University of Utah was a perfect fit for Daria to continue her academic goals, and she is now one year into her PhD under Dr. Alan Dorval, a former fellow in Dr. Grill's laboratory at Duke. In her free time, Daria enjoys creative pursuits such as knitting, sewing, and crafting furniture in addition to active pursuits such as mountaineering, skiing, and playing competitive disc golf.

Him




Collin was born in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania and spent most of his childhood near Saint Paul, Minnesota. Collin attended the Johns Hopkins University for his undergrad, where he grew to love scientific research while studying telomeres, the protective endcaps of chromosomes with Dr. Mary Armanios and Dr. Carol Greider. While Collin loved his work in genetics, he opted to pursue research more in line with his neural engineering coursework, which brought him to the University of Utah, where he is currently finishing his PhD under Dr. Alan Dorval. In his free time, Collin enjoys active pursuits such as racing marathons with a personal best time of 2:42, mountaineering, skiing, and playing competitive disc golf, as well as more creative pursuits such as playing classical piano.
